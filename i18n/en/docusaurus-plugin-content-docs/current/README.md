## Why Write a Python Book?

After all, there are already so many Python books. And besides, everyone watches videos now — who still reads books?

In China, I was among the earlier generation to start programming. Before entering university, I had a brief encounter with BASIC, though I couldn't even call it an introduction. My systematic study of programming began after college, starting with Pascal. During my student years, I also learned Assembly, C, Scheme, MATLAB, and other languages. After stepping into the workforce, the languages I primarily used were never taught in school — including C++, C#, LabVIEW, Java, PHP, JavaScript, Python, and others. In school, textbooks were our main learning resource. In my early career, when I needed to learn a new language, I would typically flip through books. Back then, finding a good book was crucial. I remember that *Effective C++* was required reading for learning C++, and *Design Patterns* guided our path in learning Java. By comparison, I picked up JavaScript and Python later, by which time learning methods had already changed — search engines and online documentation had become my primary learning resources. To my shame, I have yet to read a single book on JavaScript or Python; all my knowledge was gathered from Google searches.

Although books may no longer be our only path to knowledge, from my experience, writing — or rather, taking notes — remains an indispensable part of the learning process. Knowledge you write down with your own hands is mastered at a completely different level from knowledge you merely read. And publishing what you've written to share with others is the most powerful motivator for writing. Especially for someone as lazy as me, having such an external goal is exactly what I need to keep pushing myself to learn.

Today, we once again stand at the crest of a technological wave. Large language models are likely to replace search engines as the primary way to access learning materials in the future. Deeply experiencing the new opportunities that large language models bring to learning and writing is one of the original motivations for writing this book. After I finish organizing the core content of this book, I will share my experiences and insights on large language models in the "[Epilogue](epilogue)" section, as a conclusion to the entire writing process.
